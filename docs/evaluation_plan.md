9. This is a placeholder in the MVP and will later be replaced with an aspirational flow taking into account To Come In lists.

## Implementation status (patientflow package)

This section maps the matrix above to the current `patientflow.evaluate` API (`get_default_evaluation_targets`, `run_evaluation`). The matrix states modelling intent; not every row has a one-to-one named target or automated evaluation path.

- **Rows 5b and 7b** — The library defines targets `ed_current_window_beds` and `ed_yta_beds` as normal **distribution** evaluations when they are included in `EvaluationInputs`. Skipping them at UCLH is a **deployment choice** (omit them from the target set or from the builder); the implementations remain for other sites.

- **Rows 13, 14, and 15** — There are **no** standalone evaluation targets for the transition matrix, emergency transfer inflows, or elective transfer inflows. **Row 13** has no evaluation mode in the framework yet (see the methods table: tbc). **Rows 14 and 15** contribute only **inside** combined prediction PMFs (**rows 16–19**): `compute_transfer_arrivals` builds transfer PMFs when `FlowSelection.include_transfers_in` is true. **Row 16** and **row 18** are registered with **`aspirational_skip`** (combined emergency flows include aspirational components). **Rows 17 and 19** use **distribution** evaluation; their predicted PMFs include elective transfers, and observed counts must reflect external arrivals **and** intra-hospital transfers where applicable (see `transfers_state_of_play.md`).

- **Rows 1 and 10 (classifiers)** — `evaluate_classifier` always writes **feature importance** plots via `plot_features` → `feature_importance.png`. **SHAP** plots are emitted only when optional SHAP dependencies are installed (see `patientflow.evaluate.handlers` and `patientflow.viz.shap`).

- **Row 4b** — `evaluate_survival_curve` takes a `SurvivalCurvePayload` with **train** and **test** dataframes and plots them via `plot_admission_time_survival_curve`; it does **not** add a separate “model in use” or production curve. A third comparison series would require custom use of the survival plotting utilities or an extension to the payload.

- **Row 19** — Implemented as the `combined_net_elective` target with `evaluation_mode="distribution"` (same scalar and chart path as other specialty PMF targets), not a distinct evaluation mode.

## Evaluation methods: interpretation

This table maps each evaluation method to where its logic is implemented in `patientflow`, and summarises what a 'good' versus 'concerning' signal looks like.

| Evaluation method | Where logic is defined in code | What to look for (good) | What to look for (concerning) |
|---|---|---|---|
| AUC and AUPRC — including at cross-validation stage | `src/patientflow/train/classifiers.py` (`evaluate_predictions`, `evaluate_model`). Run-time classifier summaries are also recorded from `src/patientflow/evaluate/handlers.py` (`evaluate_classifier`). | AUC/AUPRC are high enough to show strong class separation and remain consistently high across folds. These metrics are secondary to calibration/discrimination diagnostics because downstream bed-count predictions depend on the accuracy of probability values, not just ranking ability. | AUC/AUPRC are low, or vary substantially across folds, indicating weak or unreliable discrimination. |
| Discrimination plots — pre- and post-calibration to handle imbalanced classes | `src/patientflow/viz/estimated_probabilities.py` (`plot_estimated_probabilities`) | Negative cases cluster at low predicted probabilities and positive cases at higher probabilities, with limited overlap. After recalibration, a sparse or vacant extreme right tail can be expected and is not, by itself, a problem. | Overlap increases and separation deteriorates versus the same model at the same prediction time before recalibration, and this is accompanied by poorer calibration/MADCAP or downstream bias (obs-exp/EPUDD). |
| Calibration plots | `src/patientflow/viz/calibration.py` (`plot_calibration`). Use both quantile and uniform options. | Points are close to the diagonal on the uniform calibration plot across clinically relevant probability ranges; quantile view shows the same direction of fit. | Systematic deviation from the diagonal (especially in higher-probability bins) on the uniform plot, even if the quantile plot appears smoother. Use uniform as the primary view and quantile as a robustness check. |
| MADCAP plots | `src/patientflow/viz/madcap.py` (`plot_madcap`, `plot_madcap_by_group`) | Cumulative predicted and observed lines track each other; desirable bowing pattern for discrimination. | Persistent gap between lines (predicted below observed = under-prediction of beds). Group-specific MADCAP can reveal subgroup failures. |
| Arrival rate plots | — | Differences centred near zero with balanced mass above/below and tight spread. | Skew/bias (mostly above zero = under-prediction, mostly below = over-prediction) or wide spread. |
| MAE and MPE | `src/patientflow/evaluate/legacy_api.py` (`calculate_results`, `calc_mae_mpe`) | MAE and MPE are low in absolute terms, with error magnitudes consistent with acceptable operational tolerance. Pair with obs-exp histogram and EPUDD plots to detect bias/spread that summary metrics can hide. | MAE or MPE exceed predefined acceptable limits **(what should these be?)** or imply meaningful error. Interpret alongside plots to reveal bias, skew, or spread that summary metrics alone can miss. |
| Obs-exp histogram | `src/patientflow/viz/observed_against_expected.py` (`plot_deltas`) | Differences centred near zero with balanced mass above/below and tight spread. | Skew/bias (mostly above zero = under-prediction, mostly below = over-prediction) or wide spread. |
| EPUDD | `src/patientflow/viz/epudd.py` (`plot_epudd`, helpers including `_calculate_cdf_values`) | Observed CDF points track model CDF structure closely across thresholds. | Consistent offset patterns indicating calibration bias in distribution predictions. |
| Survival curve comparison (training vs test) | Orchestration: `src/patientflow/evaluate/handlers.py` (`evaluate_survival_curve`) — **train vs test dataframes only** (`SurvivalCurvePayload`). Plotting: `src/patientflow/viz/survival_curve.py` (`plot_admission_time_survival_curve`). Curve computation: `src/patientflow/calculate/survival_curve.py` (`calculate_survival_curve`). | Train/validation/test curves are similar (no major temporal drift). | Clear gaps between curves, suggesting process drift and potential mismatch if model uses older periods. |
| Histogram of difference between cumulative arrival rates and observed numbers | `src/patientflow/viz/observed_against_expected.py` (`plot_arrival_delta_single_instance`, `plot_arrival_deltas`) | Delta trajectories hover around zero and average delta remains near zero; histogram of final deltas centred near zero. | Persistent positive/negative drift (systematic under/over prediction of arrivals) and shifted final-delta histogram. |
| Feature importances (classifiers) | `src/patientflow/viz/features.py` (`plot_features`), called from `src/patientflow/evaluate/handlers.py` (`evaluate_classifier`). | Importance rankings are stable enough across folds or times to be interpretable; they align with domain expectations where known. | Dominance by a single leaky or timestamp feature, or rankings that contradict calibration/discrimination evidence. |
| SHAP plots (classifiers) | `src/patientflow/viz/shap.py` (`plot_shap`), called from `evaluate_classifier` when optional SHAP dependencies are installed. | Local and global SHAP summaries agree with calibration and feature-importance views. | Contradictory or unstable attributions versus other diagnostics. |
